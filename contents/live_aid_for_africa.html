Live Aid for Africa

The 1985 Live Aid Concert for Africa is a charity concert set up by Bob Geldof to fund a relief fund for Ethiopia, which had been stricken by famine in 1984. Two concerts were held the same day, in London and in Philadephia, featuring artists such as David Bowie, Mick Jagger, Phil Collins, Freddy Mercury, Roger Daltrey, Paul McCartney.. It was the most ambitious international satellite television venture that had ever been attempted at the time.
